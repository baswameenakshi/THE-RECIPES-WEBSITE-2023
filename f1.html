Chicken Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tsp biryani masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

Steps:

  1. Wash and soak rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil and ghee in a large pan. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, chicken, and cook until the chicken changes color.
  4. Stir in tomatoes, biryani masala, turmeric, and cook for a few minutes.
  5. Layer half of the rice in a separate pot, add the chicken mixture, and top with the remaining rice.
  6. Add yogurt, salt, and a little water. Cover and cook until the rice is tender.
  7. Fluff the biryani, garnish with fresh coriander, and serve hot.

Nutritional Information:

Calories: Approximately 450 per serving

Protein: 20g

Carbohydrates: 40g

Fat: 20g

Gulab Jamun

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of milk powder
  • 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp of baking soda
  • 2 tbsp of ghee (clarified butter)
  • 3/4 cup of milk
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup of water
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Cardamom powder for flavor
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

Steps:

  1. Mix milk powder, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and ghee in a bowl.
  2. Add milk slowly to form a soft and smooth dough. Let it rest for 10 minutes.
  3. In a separate pot, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, saffron strands, and cardamom powder until it reaches one-string consistency.
  4. Divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and roll them into smooth balls, ensuring no cracks.
  5. Heat oil or ghee in a pan and deep fry the dough balls until they turn golden brown, maintaining low to medium heat throughout.
  6. Remove fried balls and soak them in the prepared sugar syrup for at least an hour.
  7. Your Gulab Jamun is ready to serve after soaking and absorbing the sugar syrup.

Nutritional Information:

Calories: Approximately 150 per serving (2 Gulab Jamun)

Protein: 2g

Carbohydrates: 30g

Fat: 3g

Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • Various toppings (e.g., chocolate chips, nuts, fruit, sprinkles)

Steps:

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together heavy cream and sugar until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Stir in the whole milk and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  4. When the ice cream is almost done, add your choice of toppings and mix for another minute or two.
  5. Transfer the ice cream to a lidded container and freeze for several hours or until firm.
  6. Scoop and serve your homemade ice cream with additional toppings if desired.

Nutritional Information:

Calories: Approximately 250 per serving

Protein: 2g

Carbohydrates: 20g

Fat: 18g

Manchurian

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1/2 cup carrot, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon red chili sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornstarch, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, red chili sauce, salt, and pepper to form a thick batter.
  2. Dip cauliflower florets, carrot, and cabbage into the batter, ensuring they are well coated.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the coated vegetables until they turn golden brown.
  4. Remove and drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  5. Manchurian is ready to serve as an appetizer or with sauce.

Nutritional Information:

Calories: Approximately 180 per serving

Protein: 4g

Carbohydrates: 20g

Fat: 10g

Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cold)
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, carrots, bell peppers)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pan and sauté garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add mixed vegetables and cook until slightly tender.
  3. Push vegetables to one side of the pan and pour beaten eggs into the other side. Scramble the eggs and mix with the vegetables.
  4. Add cold cooked rice and soy sauce. Stir-fry until well combined and heated through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot.

Nutritional Information:

Calories: Approximately 300 per serving

Protein: 8g

Carbohydrates: 45g

Fat: 10g

  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 green chili, chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • A few curry leaves
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Steps:

  1. Roast the vermicelli in a dry pan until it turns golden brown. Set aside.

  5. Add the roasted vermicelli, reduce the heat, and cover. Let it cook for about 10 minutes or until the vermicelli is soft and cooked through.
  6. Fluff the Semiya with a fork, and your dish is ready to serve.

Kala Jamun

Ingredients:

  • 200g khoya (milk solids)
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • Ghee or oil for frying

Steps:

  1. In a mixing bowl, crumble the khoya until it's smooth and without any lumps.
  2. Add all-purpose flour, cardamom powder, and baking soda to the khoya. Mix until the dough comes together. If the dough is too dry, you can add a few drops of milk to moisten it.
  3. Divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and shape them into smooth balls. You can also stuff each ball with a saffron strand if desired.
  4. Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Fry the khoya balls until they turn dark brown or "kala" on medium-low heat. Be patient and cook them evenly. Remove and set them aside.
  5. In a separate pan, prepare the sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water until it reaches a one-string consistency. Remove it from heat.
  6. Soak the fried khoya balls in the sugar syrup for a few hours to allow them to absorb the syrup and soften.
  7. Your Kala Jamun is ready to be served once they have soaked in the syrup and become deliciously sweet.

Per serving (approximately 1 piece)

  • Protein: 1-2g
  • Carbohydrates: 10-15g
  • Fat: 6-9g

Rasmalai

Ingredients:

  • 8 pieces of rasgulla (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios and almonds
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands (optional, for garnish)

Steps:

  1. In a wide pan, heat the milk and bring it to a boil. Stir frequently to avoid sticking to the bottom.
  2. Lower the heat and add sugar, cardamom powder, and chopped nuts. Simmer and stir occasionally until the milk thickens and reduces to about half its original volume.
  3. While the milk is simmering, gently squeeze excess sugar syrup from the rasgullas. You can also flatten them slightly to allow the flavored milk to penetrate better.
  4. Once the milk is thickened, remove it from heat and let it cool to room temperature. You can refrigerate it to speed up the cooling process.
  5. Place the squeezed rasgullas in the cooled milk. Garnish with saffron strands and more chopped nuts.
  6. Refrigerate for a few hours or until well chilled. Your Rasmalai is ready to serve once the rasgullas have absorbed the flavored milk.

Per serving (approximately 1 piece)

Rasgulla

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands (optional, for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Boil the milk in a large pan. Once it comes to a boil, add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle the milk. Stir gently until the whey separates from the curd.
  2. Strain the curdled milk through a muslin cloth or fine sieve. Rinse the chenna (paneer) under cold water to remove any trace of lemon juice or vinegar. Squeeze out excess water from the chenna.
  3. Knead the chenna well until it becomes smooth and no longer grainy. Divide it into small, equal-sized portions and roll them into smooth balls, without any cracks.
  4. In a separate pot, combine sugar and water. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer. Add the cardamom powder.
  5. Drop the chenna balls into the simmering sugar syrup. Cover the pot and cook for about 20-25 minutes until the rasgullas have doubled in size and are spongy.
  6. Remove the rasgullas from the sugar syrup and allow them to cool. Garnish with saffron strands if desired.
  7. Your Rasgulla is ready to be served chilled or at room temperature.

Nutritional Information:

Per serving (approximately 1 rasgulla)

  • Calories: 100-150
  • Protein: 2-3g
  • Carbohydrates: 20-25g
  • Fat: 0.5-1g
  • Fiber: 0g

Mutton Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 500g mutton, washed and cleaned
  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • Whole spices (e.g., cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, bay leaf)
  • Ghee or cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Marinate mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, turmeric, and salt. Let it sit for at least 1-2 hours.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee or cooking oil. Add whole spices and sliced onions. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add the marinated mutton and cook until it's partially done. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, and mint-coriander leaves. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  4. In a separate pot, parboil the soaked rice. Drain and set aside.
  5. Layer the partially cooked mutton with the parboiled rice in the pot. Sprinkle saffron milk on top for color and aroma.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat (dum) for about 30-40 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors meld together.
  7. Your Mutton Biryani is ready to serve. Garnish with additional mint-coriander leaves if desired.

Nutritional Information:

Per serving (approximately 200-250g)

  • Calories: 400-500
  • Protein: 12-15g
  • Carbohydrates: 40-50g
  • Fat: 15-20g
  • Fiber: 2-3g
